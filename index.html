About Me Hands-on learner building a career in IT support and identity access management — focused on real labs, real systems, and real value

Get to know me!

When I first started exploring tech, I felt overwhelmed. There were so many areas to learn, and my curiosity pulled me in every direction. I wanted to learn everything. Front end, back end. If there had been a side end, I probably would have tried that too. I was learning a lot, but not in a focused way, which hurt my comprehension of the material.

About two years ago, I created a more realistic plan. I started with the Google Cybersecurity and IT Support certificates. Then I earned my CompTIA A+ and began studying for the Network+ to strengthen my technical foundation.

As I looked for realistic entry points into the field, help desk and technical support roles stood out. System administration seemed to build on the same skills, but at a deeper level. I kept seeing familiar tools in job listings like Windows, Active Directory, virtualization, and password management.

That led me to identity and access management. When I first learned about AAA (Authentication, Authorization, and Accounting), it didn’t seem as exciting as pentesting or working in a SOC. But look at me now. I like IAM because it is administrative and balances structure and problem-solving. It allows me to write scripts and automate tasks, while also applying logic, organization, and big-picture thinking to keep systems running smoothly.

This year, most of my work has centered on Microsoft Entra. I set up my own Azure subscription and started documenting labs to build real-world experience. For example, I created and managed users in Entra ID, configured RBAC, and set up a lab simulating group policy scenarios. While my work is Microsoft centered, I believe the skills I have acquired can carry over to any platform.

IAM connects everything. It plays a role in cybersecurity, system administration, compliance, and even DevOps. That overlap is what makes it so important. It’s an area where I can make an impact early on and continue building long-term, valuable skills that apply across the entire IT landscape.
